The CSRS collects, uses and discloses your information in the following ways and for the following purposes:

  1. INFORMATION COLLECTION AND USE
    • Recalculation Information - you will only be required to provide information through the Service Portal that is necessary to assist with a Recalculation.

The CSRS is authorized by law to collect the following types of information from you and the other parent (“Other Parent”) of any child(ren) (“Child”) of yours and the Other Parent (you and the Other Parent are each referred to in these Terms as a “Party” and collectively as the “Parties”):

  • contact information for each Party, including name, address, phone number and email address (“Contact Information”)
  • the date of birth for each Party
  • the name(s) and date(s) of birth for any Child named in a child support order or written agreement (“Child Information”)
  • each Party’s financial information, including Social Insurance Number, annual income and filed income tax documentation, such as personal income tax returns and Notice of Assessment/Reassessment, in accordance with the Family Law Act Regulation
  • written agreements between the Parties

Any of the above information that you provide through the Service Portal will be linked to your CSRS file (“CSRS File”) for the purposes of facilitating our consideration of a Recalculation. 

We may also use Contact Information to inform a Party:

  • to log into the Service Portal to receive messages or to receive or provide updates about a Recalculation
  • of any changes to these Terms
    • Other CSRS File Information - the CSRS may collect information from a Party through the Service Portal about special circumstances relating to their CSRS File, including:
  • the existence of any Family Maintenance Enforcement Program (“FMEP”) file with the British Columbia Family Maintenance Agency (“BCFMA”) to ensure BCFMA is aware of any changes to a payor’s child support obligations, which may impact enforcement
  • whether there are any safety concerns for a Party or any Child (“Safety Concerns”) to manage risk for a protected party enrolled in the CSRS
  • circumstances regarding any Child over the age of majority to determine if the CSRS is able to make a recalculation based on the Child being over the age of majority
    • Optional Information - you may also be asked to:
  • provide certain additional user information, including your gender, ethnicity and how you heard about the CSRS
  • participate in a survey about your overall experience with the CSRS Recalculation process, which may include questions about Service Portal functionality

If you decide to provide any of the above optional information, it will be used to help improve CSRS’ provision of the Services and users' experience with the Service Portal.

Your decision not to provide any of the above optional information will not impact your access to or use of the Services.

  1. INFORMATION DISCLOSURE
  • To the Other Parent - the CSRS is legally authorized to, and may, disclose the following types of information to the Other Parent:
  • Child Information
  • annual or deemed income (“Income”)
  • the amount of child support payable by a Party (“Support Payable”)
  • information derived from orders of the court (“Court Orders”)
  • date(s) and location(s) of any court hearings
  • any CSRS “Statement of Recalculation”
  • notice that a Party has filed an application with the court to dispute a Statement of Recalculation
  • notice that a Party has submitted a request to withdraw from the Recalculation process
  • To the BCFMA - the CSRS is legally authorized to, and may, disclose the following types of information to the BCFMA, if a Party is enrolled with the FMEP:
  • Child Information
  • Income
  • Support Payable
  • Contact Information
  • Statement of Recalculation
  • issuance of a “Notice to Suspend Recalculation” due to a dispute application being filed by a Party
  • changes affecting amounts due for a Child under the age of majority, including that:
  • a Child is now living with payor
  • the recipient has agreed to accept less support
  • a Child is deceased
  • changes affecting the FMEP case or CSRS File, including that:
  • the payor or recipient is deceased
  • the recipient has withdrawn from FMEP
  • the Parties have withdrawn a Recalculation request
  • either Party leaves British Columbia
  • the Parties have reconciled
  • that a Court Order becomes ineligible or has been declined for Recalculation
  • Safety Concerns being reported by a payor or recipient
  • To the Court - the CSRS is legally authorized to, and may, disclose the following types of information to the Provincial Court of British Columbia:
  • Child Information
  • Income
  • Support Payable
  • Contact Information
  • Statement of Recalculation
